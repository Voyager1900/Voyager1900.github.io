Liquid Entropy Interactive Experience Research

1) What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I notice is lighting shifting when I use mouse to interact.

2) Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  1. Open the url
  2. Observe the scene appeared
  3. Mouse stay on the website
  4. Mouse moving to the left and to the right, up and down
  5. Observe the way of the light moving on the website
  6. Moving the mouse to follow the light
  7. Read what’s on the control panel
  8. Turn the mouse size down; Moving the mouse circling around on the page
  9. Turn the speed up
  10. Moving the mouse circling around on the page
  11. Increase the delay feedback
  12. Moving the mouse to stay on the page

3) What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

Moving the mouse and observe the effect it creates.

4) What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

Try moving the mouse from different directions and at different speeds.

5) What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

Let the audience try out different control settings, and engage with the whole aesthetic experience.

6) How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The whole page is subjected to interactive change, so audience will most inevitably hover their mouse somewhere in the page and naturally engage in the interaction.

7) What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

I find out the experience should be interacted with mouse movement almost immediately, and then after about half a minute I noticed there’s a control panel on the top right side.

8) How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The audience will find out very soon that each time they move the mouse, they create a totally different image, and each moment distinguished from the all the other. This gives the audience the motivation to experiment with the control options once they notice it.

9) What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The experience refers moving image.

10) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

Just moving the mouse.

11) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

Openminded and being creative, be experimental.

12) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

When I look at the effect I create on this website for too long and I feel a lit bit dizzy, especially when I turn the speed up in the control panel. It would be better if they give for example a warning sign when click into this section of the website for people with conditions like epilepsy.

13) What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

I find the effect after I stop moving the mouse satisfying, my screen becomes a melting mirror and I can see the ripple effect I created and see if that’s what expected.
